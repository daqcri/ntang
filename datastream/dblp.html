Clique Problem

It is to find a group of authors who write papers with each other more regularly than others.
In the right, we show the most frequent 1, 2 and 3 cliques.

Heavy nodes

Coauthor graph

A coauthor graph is an undirected and edge weighted graph. Each node is an author. Each edge between two authors means that they published some papers together, and the edge weight indicates the number of papers they published.

One-hop weight

The one-hop weight of a node is the aggregated weight of all its edges.

Two-hop weight

The two-hop weight of a node v is the aggregated weight of all nodes that are reachable from v in 2 hops.

Challenges

What if the data is in streaming fashion?

Listing all 3 cliques (or triangles) is a hard problem in data streams.
No known solutions exists to find two-hop heavy hitters.

So why is it hard in data streams?

1-pass of the data
You may not able to store all the data
You have to do it fast (linear time?) !!!
