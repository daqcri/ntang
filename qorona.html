COVID-19: An Analysis of Cases in Qatar

On 2020 March 11, the sudden announcement of 238 new cases in Qatar shocked everyone, making the confirmed numbers jumped from 24 to 262. I will use this page to keep track of and analyze what is happening in Qatar in fighting against COVID-19. I really hope that I can keep this as simple as possible, without any complicated new cases to analyze.

Some Analysis

Except the spike on March 11 that had 238 new cases, Qatar did not have the short period of exponential increase in terms of the new cases (the yellow line) afterwards, as has been observed by many other countries. Thanks the prompt actions of Qatar government in its efforts to controlling the spread of the virus.

Actions

03-18: The closure of malls, shopping centers, barbershops, and beauty salons.
03-17: All mosques closed amid coronavirus (COVID) in Qatar till further notice
03-17: MOI announces the road closure of the old industrial street
03-16: Only Qatari citizens allowed entry into the country effective immediately
03-15: MOCI: dining in restaurants and cafes to be suspended
03-15: Incoming flights to Doha to be stopped for 14 days; public transportation to be stopped
03-12: MME announces closure of children's play areas in parks
03-11: Coronavirus (COVID-19) declared a pandemic: WHO
03-09: GCO: Suspension of schools and universities due to coronavirus
