Research: Data Preparation for All

My research interests center around data preparation, a field that I have been working on for a decade. The practical need on this topic is evident -- an oft-cited statistic is that data scientists spend at least 80% of their time on data preparation.

Generally speaking, data preparation includes discovering datasets from a large data repository such as data lakes, integrating the discovered datasets from multiple sources into a single and unified dataset, enriching a dataset with other datasets, cleaning the dataset by correcting erroneous values or imputing missing values, and transforming the data into a uniform representation, prior to downstream analytical tasks, such as data mining and machine learning.

Building tools to effectively and efficiently support data preparation in the era of big data faces more challenges than ever, simply because of its high and increasing volume, variety and velocity of data in many applications. Moreover, a recent trend in the ML community is from model-centric to data-centric, which emphasizes the importance of high quality data and labels for improving model performance.

Despite its increasing importance and the rich theoretical and practical contributions in all aspects of data preparation, it remains a lengthy undertaking for data professionals or business users, which also typically requires human- or crowd-in-the-loop. Nevertheless, it is essential as a prerequisite for all data science tasks.

My studies on data preparation can be categorized into four main themes:

  • Traditional data preparation
  • Deep learning for data preparation
  • Data preparation for machine learning
  • Visualization for data preparation
