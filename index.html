Research Interests

An oft-cited statistic is that data scientists spend at least 80% of their time on data preparation, including discovering datasets from a large data repository such as data lakes, integrating the discovered datasets from multiple sources into a single and unified dataset, enriching a dataset with other datasets, cleaning the dataset by correcting erroneous values or imputing missing values, and transforming the data into a uniform representation, prior to downstream analytical tasks, such as data mining and machine learning. In particular, a recent trend in the ML community is from model-centric to data-centric, which emphasizes the importance of data preparation for improving model performance.

Despite the increasing importance of data preparation and the rich theoretical and practical contributions in all aspects of data preparation, it remains a lengthy undertaking for data professionals or business users, which also typically requires human- or crowd-in-the-loop. Nevertheless, it is essential as a prerequisite for all data science tasks. Furthermore, solving data preparation in big data faces more challenges due to its high and increasing volume, variety and velocity of data in many applications.

My main research interest is about data preparation, including the following aspects:

  • Traditional data preparation
  • Deep learning for data preparation
  • Data preparation for machine learning
  • Visualization for data preparation
